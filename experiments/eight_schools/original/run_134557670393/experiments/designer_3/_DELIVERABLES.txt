================================================================================
THREE PROPOSED MODELS
================================================================================

MODEL 1: ROBUST STUDENT-T META-ANALYSIS (RSTMA)
├── Core Idea: Heavy-tailed likelihood for outlier robustness
├── Key Parameter: nu (degrees of freedom)
├── Complexity: Medium
├── Runtime: ~5-10 minutes
├── Priority: HIGH (recommended first choice)
├── Best For: Study 1 influence, distributional robustness
└── Abandon If: nu > 50 (converges to Normal)

MODEL 2: FINITE MIXTURE META-ANALYSIS (TMMA)
├── Core Idea: Two latent groups with different effects
├── Key Parameter: pi (mixing proportion)
├── Complexity: High
├── Runtime: ~10-20 minutes
├── Priority: MEDIUM (may not identify with J=8)
├── Best For: Explicit subgroup structure, clustering
└── Abandon If: Groups collapse (pi extreme) or not separated

MODEL 3: UNCERTAINTY-INFLATED META-ANALYSIS (UIMA)
├── Core Idea: Reported SEs may be underestimated
├── Key Parameter: lambda (inflation factor)
├── Complexity: Low
├── Runtime: ~2-5 minutes
├── Priority: MEDIUM (easy robustness check)
├── Best For: SE quality doubts, conservative inference
└── Abandon If: lambda ≈ 1 (no inflation needed)

================================================================================
PHILOSOPHY
================================================================================

"Models should fail informatively. Abandoning a model that doesn't fit the 
data is SUCCESS, not failure. The goal is finding truth, not completing tasks."

Key Principles:
  - Design for falsification (explicit abandonment criteria)
  - Robust methods (conservative inference)
  - Small sample awareness (J=8 has limitations)
  - Honest uncertainty reporting (data may be insufficient)
  - Truth-finding over task-completion

================================================================================
QUALITY ASSURANCE
================================================================================

Each model must pass:
  ✓ R-hat < 1.01 (convergence)
  ✓ ESS > 400 (sufficient samples)
  ✓ Divergences < 10 (geometry okay)
  ✓ PPC > 80% coverage (fit quality)
  ✓ LOO Pareto k < 0.7 (predictive validity)
  ✓ Falsification checks passed (model appropriate)
  ✓ Sensitivity analyses robust (not prior-driven)
